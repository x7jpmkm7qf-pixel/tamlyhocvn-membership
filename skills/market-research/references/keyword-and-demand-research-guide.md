# Keyword and Demand Research Guide

Systematic workflow for identifying high-demand, low-competition keywords and validating market opportunities through search behavior analysis.

## Overview

This guide walks you through five research phases that take you from initial idea to validated niche with quantified demand. Budget 6-8 hours for thorough first-time research, 3-4 hours for subsequent analyses.

## Phase 1: Seed Keyword Brainstorming

Generate initial keyword list using multiple discovery methods.

### Method 1: Problem-Based Discovery
Start with problems you can solve:
1. List 5 core problems in your domain
2. For each problem, write 3 ways people describe it
3. Add industry jargon + plain language versions

**Example (Digital Marketing)**:
- Problem: "Getting website traffic"
- Variants: "increase website visitors", "drive more traffic", "boost site visits", "organic traffic growth", "SEO traffic strategies"

### Method 2: Audience Language Mining
Extract actual phrases your audience uses:
1. Visit 5 relevant Reddit threads or Facebook groups
2. Copy 10 questions or complaints verbatim
3. Identify recurring phrases and terminology
4. Note emotional triggers ("frustrated with...", "struggling to...")

### Method 3: Competitor Keyword Reverse Engineering
Learn from existing players:
1. Identify 3 successful competitors
2. Review their page titles, blog post headlines, course names
3. List repeated themes and keywords
4. Note gaps in their coverage

### Method 4: Category Expansion
Branch out systematically:
1. Start with main topic (e.g., "content marketing")
2. Add qualifiers: beginner/advanced, cheap/premium, fast/comprehensive
3. Add use cases: for freelancers/agencies/startups
4. Add formats: course/template/tool/service
5. Add outcomes: certification/portfolio/income

### Method 5: Autocomplete Harvesting
Let search engines guide you:
1. Type seed keyword in Google
2. Record all autocomplete suggestions
3. Add alphabet soup: "keyword a", "keyword b", etc.
4. Check "People Also Ask" section
5. Review "Related Searches" at bottom

**Phase 1 Target**: 50-100 seed keywords across all methods

## Phase 2: Google Trends Analysis

Validate trend direction and identify seasonal patterns.

### Setting Up Your Analysis
1. Go to trends.google.com
2. Set geography to your target market (Vietnam, US, Global)
3. Set time range to "Past 5 years" for trend direction
4. Compare 3-5 related keywords at once

### Reading Trend Signals

**Strong Signals** (Pursue):
- Steady upward trajectory over 2+ years
- Consistent baseline even in low periods
- Sharp spikes with sustained elevation afterward
- Regional breakout opportunities (high in specific locations)

**Caution Signals** (Investigate Further):
- Flat line with no growth but steady interest
- Seasonal spikes with complete drops to zero
- High volatility without clear pattern
- Declining trend over past year

**Red Flags** (Avoid):
- Consistent downward trend
- Single spike then return to near-zero
- Replaced by alternative terminology
- No data or insufficient search volume

### Comparative Analysis Method
1. Plot your top 10 keywords against each other
2. Identify the rising star (growing fastest)
3. Check related queries for expansion opportunities
4. Examine breakout searches for emerging sub-niches

### Seasonal Pattern Detection
For each keyword:
1. Switch to "Past 12 months" view
2. Note peak and trough months
3. Calculate seasonality ratio: (Peak / Average)
4. Plan content calendar around patterns

**High Seasonality (>3x)**: Time-sensitive, prepare 2 months ahead
**Moderate Seasonality (1.5-3x)**: Optimize for peaks, maintain year-round
**Low Seasonality (<1.5x)**: Evergreen content, consistent publishing

### Geographic Opportunity Spotting
1. Review "Interest by region" section
2. Identify unexpectedly high-interest locations
3. Check local competition levels
4. Consider localization opportunities

**Phase 2 Output**: Trends report showing growth direction, seasonality patterns, and geographic concentrations for top 20 keywords

## Phase 3: Keyword Planner Deep-Dive

Quantify search demand and assess competition levels.

### Accessing the Tool
1. Create free Google Ads account (no spending required)
2. Navigate to Tools → Planning → Keyword Planner
3. Select "Discover new keywords"
4. Enter your seed keywords from Phase 1

### Interpreting Search Volume

**Volume Ranges and Opportunity**:
- **10-100/month**: Micro-niche, ultra-specific, easy to rank, limited scale
- **100-1,000/month**: Small niche, focused audience, moderate potential
- **1,000-10,000/month**: Sweet spot for solopreneurs, good balance
- **10,000-100,000/month**: High demand, intense competition, requires resources
- **100,000+/month**: Broad market, difficult entry, established players dominate

**Rule of Thumb**: For first ventures, target 1,000-10,000 monthly searches in primary keyword, supplemented by 10+ supporting keywords with 100-1,000 searches each.

### Competition Level Analysis

Tool shows "Low/Medium/High" competition for ads:

**Low Competition**:
- Few advertisers bidding (demand validation concern)
- OR: B2B niches with long sales cycles (opportunity)
- Action: Verify demand through other signals

**Medium Competition**:
- Healthy advertiser interest
- Proven willingness to pay
- Not oversaturated
- **Ideal zone for most creators**

**High Competition**:
- Many advertisers, high bids
- Strong monetization potential
- But: requires significant budget or expertise
- Consider sub-niches with medium competition

### CPC as Demand Indicator

Cost-per-click reveals commercial intent:

**$0.10-$0.50**: Low commercial value
- Informational searches
- Difficult to monetize directly
- Build audience plays

**$0.50-$2.00**: Moderate value
- Some buying intent
- Monetization possible
- Good for affiliates and courses

**$2.00-$10.00**: High value
- Strong commercial intent
- Proven buyer audience
- **Target zone for service providers**

**$10.00+**: Premium markets
- High-ticket sales
- B2B opportunities
- Requires expert positioning

### Related Keywords Expansion
1. Review "Keyword ideas" tab
2. Sort by relevance, then volume
3. Identify clusters around themes
4. Export top 100 keywords per seed term
5. Create keyword groups by intent:
   - **Informational**: "how to", "what is", "guide to"
   - **Commercial**: "best", "top", "review", "vs"
   - **Transactional**: "buy", "price", "hire", "course"

### Building Your Keyword Portfolio

Create balanced mix:
- **1 Primary Head Term**: 5,000-20,000 searches, medium competition
- **5-10 Secondary Terms**: 1,000-5,000 searches, low-medium competition
- **20-30 Long-Tail Terms**: 100-1,000 searches, low competition
- **50+ Supporting Terms**: 10-100 searches, ultra-targeted

**Total Addressable Search Volume**: Sum all keywords = monthly opportunity ceiling

**Phase 3 Output**: Spreadsheet with keywords, volumes, competition, CPC, grouped by intent and theme

## Phase 4: Marketplace Signal Analysis

Validate demand through actual buying behavior on platforms.

### E-Commerce Marketplaces (Shopee, Tiki, Lazada)

**Analysis Steps**:
1. Search your keyword in marketplace
2. Record number of results
3. Note top seller metrics:
   - Units sold (if visible)
   - Review count and ratings
   - Price points and ranges
   - Seller types (brands vs individuals)

**Demand Signals**:
- **Strong**: 100+ products, top sellers with 1,000+ sales, 4.5+ ratings
- **Moderate**: 20-100 products, several hundred sales on top items
- **Weak**: <20 products, low sales counts, mixed reviews

**Opportunity Indicators**:
- High demand but low-quality products (poor reviews)
- Narrow price range (room for premium/budget options)
- Few informational resources (opportunity for education)
- Recent category emergence (trending section)

### Course Marketplaces (Udemy, Skillshare, Coursera)

**Analysis Steps**:
1. Search keyword in course platform
2. Sort by "Most Popular" or "Best Selling"
3. Review top 10 courses:
   - Enrollment numbers
   - Review counts and ratings
   - Pricing
   - Last update date
   - Instructor credentials

**Demand Validation**:
- **Proven Demand**: Top course has 10,000+ students, 4.0+ rating
- **Growing Demand**: Recent courses (6 months) gaining traction
- **Saturated**: Dozens of courses, similar content, price wars
- **Opportunity**: High demand but poor ratings (quality gap)

**Content Gap Analysis**:
1. Read 1-star and 2-star reviews
2. Note recurring complaints
3. Check curriculum for missing topics
4. Identify underserved skill levels (beginner vs advanced)

### Social Media and Community Platforms

**Hashtag Volume Analysis**:
1. Search keyword as hashtag on Instagram, TikTok, Twitter
2. Record post counts:
   - **10K-100K**: Micro-community, engaged but small
   - **100K-1M**: Active niche, good engagement potential
   - **1M-10M**: Established topic, high competition
   - **10M+**: Mainstream, requires differentiation

**Engagement Rate Check**:
1. Review top posts in hashtag
2. Calculate: (Likes + Comments) / Followers
3. **3%+**: Highly engaged audience (strong signal)
4. **1-3%**: Normal engagement
5. **<1%**: Passive audience or bot activity

**Facebook Group Analysis**:
1. Search keyword in Groups
2. Identify 5-10 relevant groups
3. Record member counts and activity:
   - **Daily Posts**: Active, engaged community
   - **Weekly Posts**: Moderate interest
   - **Monthly**: Dormant or poorly managed

**Reddit Subreddit Investigation**:
1. Find 3-5 related subreddits
2. Check subscriber counts and "users here now"
3. Review top posts from past month
4. Note question frequency and type
5. Calculate active percentage: Online / Total (5%+ = very active)

### Content Platform Signals (YouTube, Blogs, Podcasts)

**YouTube Analysis**:
1. Search keyword
2. Filter by upload date (past year)
3. Count videos and total views
4. Check subscriber counts of top creators
5. Review video performance vs channel size

**Signals**:
- Small channels (10K subs) getting 50K+ views = high demand
- Many recent uploads = trending topic
- High view-to-subscriber ratio = strong interest

**Blog Content Analysis**:
1. Google search: "keyword + blog"
2. Review top 10 results
3. Note publication dates
4. Check social shares (BuzzSumo or SharedCount)
5. Assess content depth and quality

**Phase 4 Output**: Marketplace validation report with platform-specific metrics and buying behavior evidence

## Phase 5: Demand Scoring Matrix

Synthesize all research into prioritized niche ranking.

### Scoring Criteria (5 Dimensions)

#### 1. Search Volume Score (0-20 points)
- **20 pts**: Primary keyword 10,000+ searches, portfolio 50,000+ total
- **15 pts**: Primary 5,000-10,000, portfolio 20,000-50,000
- **10 pts**: Primary 1,000-5,000, portfolio 5,000-20,000
- **5 pts**: Primary 500-1,000, portfolio 2,000-5,000
- **0 pts**: Primary <500 or insufficient portfolio depth

#### 2. Trend Direction Score (0-20 points)
- **20 pts**: Strong upward trend, 50%+ growth over 2 years
- **15 pts**: Moderate growth, 20-50% increase
- **10 pts**: Stable/flat, consistent interest
- **5 pts**: Slight decline but large absolute volume
- **0 pts**: Clear downward trend or fad spike

#### 3. Competition Level Score (0-20 points)
- **20 pts**: Low competition, clear differentiation opportunities
- **15 pts**: Medium competition, proven demand, space for quality
- **10 pts**: High competition but identifiable gaps
- **5 pts**: Very high competition, requires significant resources
- **0 pts**: Dominated by major brands, no entry path

#### 4. Monetization Potential Score (0-20 points)
Evaluate across multiple models:

**Direct Sales (Courses, Products, Services)**:
- CPC $5+ or proven marketplace sales = 7 pts
- CPC $2-5 or moderate sales = 5 pts
- CPC $0.50-2 or emerging market = 3 pts

**Affiliate Opportunities**:
- Major programs with 10%+ commission = 6 pts
- Some programs, 5-10% commission = 4 pts
- Limited programs or low commission = 2 pts

**Long-term Value**:
- Subscription/recurring revenue potential = 7 pts
- One-time but high-ticket ($500+) = 5 pts
- Low-ticket one-time only = 2 pts

**Total**: Sum all applicable models (max 20)

#### 5. Personal Alignment Score (0-20 points)

**Expertise Level**:
- Expert/professional experience = 8 pts
- Intermediate knowledge = 5 pts
- Beginner but passionate learner = 2 pts

**Interest Sustainability**:
- Deep passion, could work on for years = 8 pts
- Strong interest, 1-2 years = 5 pts
- Moderate interest, tactical opportunity = 2 pts

**Network Access**:
- Direct access to target audience = 4 pts
- Indirect connections = 2 pts
- No existing network = 0 pts

### Final Scoring and Decision Framework

**Total Score: 0-100 points**

**80-100 (Exceptional Opportunity)**:
- Strong proceed signal
- Allocate significant resources
- Fast-track development
- Build for long-term

**60-79 (Solid Opportunity)**:
- Green light with standard caution
- Validate with MVP first
- Monitor metrics closely
- Scale if early traction

**40-59 (Marginal Opportunity)**:
- Proceed only if strong personal alignment (16+ pts)
- Keep investment minimal
- Set clear success milestones
- Exit plan if benchmarks not met

**Below 40 (Avoid)**:
- Do not pursue
- Return to Phase 1
- Consider adjacent niches
- Learn from research process

## Research Best Practices

### Data Collection
- **Save Everything**: Screenshot charts, export data, archive web pages
- **Date Everything**: Markets change quickly, timestamp all findings
- **Source Everything**: Note where each data point originated
- **Version Control**: Create research_v1, v2, etc. as you update

### Bias Prevention
- **Actively Seek Disconfirmation**: Look for reasons NOT to pursue
- **Third-Party Validation**: Show research to someone unfamiliar with niche
- **Check Assumptions**: Question every "I think" with "What proves this?"
- **Devil's Advocate**: Argue against your favorite niche

### Time Management
- **Set Hard Deadlines**: Research can consume infinite time
- **Timebox Phases**: Phase 1 = 2hrs, Phase 2 = 1.5hrs, etc.
- **Good Enough > Perfect**: 80% confidence is sufficient to validate with MVP
- **Batch Similar Tasks**: Do all Trends analysis at once, all Keyword Planner together

### Iteration Strategy
- **Start Wide**: Research 10-15 niches simultaneously in Phase 1-2
- **Narrow Progressively**: Cut 50% after each phase
- **Deep-Dive Final 2-3**: Full scoring matrix only for top candidates
- **Keep Backup Options**: Second-place niche may become first if MVP fails

## Common Research Mistakes

1. **Confusing Interest with Demand**: High search volume ≠ willingness to pay
2. **Ignoring Competition**: Competitors validate demand and provide learning opportunities
3. **Overlooking Seasonality**: Holiday-dependent niches require different strategy
4. **Trusting Single Sources**: Always triangulate across 3+ data points
5. **Analysis Paralysis**: Set decision deadline before starting research
6. **Skipping Personal Fit**: External opportunity without internal alignment = burnout
7. **Forgetting Validation**: Research predicts, only market testing confirms

## Rapid Research Protocol (2-Hour Sprint)

When you need quick validation:

**Hour 1: Quick Discovery**
- 0-15min: Generate 10 seed keywords (autocomplete + Reddit)
- 15-30min: Google Trends check (trend direction only)
- 30-45min: Keyword Planner (volume + competition)
- 45-60min: Marketplace scan (top products/courses)

**Hour 2: Score and Decide**
- 60-75min: Social proof check (hashtags + groups)
- 75-90min: Competition quick scan (top 5 Google results)
- 90-105min: Score on simplified matrix (3 criteria: volume, trend, competition)
- 105-120min: Decision + outline next steps

**Output**: Go/no-go decision with 70% confidence

Use this for initial filtering before full research on finalists.
