# Market Analysis Framework for Solopreneurs

## Overview
Simplified frameworks for validating market opportunities without corporate research budgets. Designed for one-person businesses in Vietnam.

---

## 1. PESTEL Analysis (Simplified)

### What It Is
6-factor environmental scan to identify opportunities and threats.

### When to Use It
- Entering new market/niche
- Launching new product category
- Annual strategic review

### The 6 Factors

#### P — Political
Questions:
- Are there new regulations affecting your niche? (e.g., data privacy, licensing)
- Government support programs for digital businesses?
- Changes in taxation for online businesses?

**Vietnam Context:**
- E-commerce law changes
- Social media advertising regulations
- Cross-border payment restrictions

#### E — Economic
Questions:
- Is disposable income rising/falling in target segment?
- Exchange rate impacts on pricing (if using international tools)?
- Inflation affecting purchasing power?

**Vietnam Context:**
- Middle class expansion
- Digital payment adoption rate
- Youth unemployment rate

#### S — Social
Questions:
- Shifting attitudes toward online learning/digital products?
- Demographic trends (aging, urbanization)?
- Lifestyle changes (remote work, side hustles)?

**Vietnam Context:**
- Gen Z digital natives entering workforce
- Growing acceptance of "học online"
- Side hustle culture boom

#### T — Technological
Questions:
- New platforms emerging? (e.g., TikTok Shop)
- Tool accessibility improving? (free/cheap software)
- Internet/smartphone penetration changes?

**Vietnam Context:**
- 5G rollout impact
- AI tools democratization
- Mobile-first behavior (95%+ use phones)

#### E — Environmental
Questions:
- Sustainability trends affecting purchasing decisions?
- Remote work reducing need for physical products?

**Vietnam Context:**
- Growing eco-consciousness in urban areas
- Digital-first reducing paper/physical product demand

#### L — Legal
Questions:
- Copyright/IP laws affecting content creation?
- Consumer protection laws changing?
- Contract/payment laws for digital goods?

**Vietnam Context:**
- Stricter content moderation on platforms
- Invoice/receipt requirements for online sales
- Warranty laws for digital products

```markdown

- Opportunity/Threat:
- Action:

### Economic
- Key finding 1:
- Opportunity/Threat:
- Action:

[Repeat for S, T, E, L]

### Top 3 Priorities:
1. [Action based on biggest opportunity]
2. [Action to mitigate biggest threat]
3. [Action to leverage emerging trend]
```

## 2. Five Forces Analysis (Competitive Assessment)

### What It Is
Assess competitive intensity and profitability potential using 5 factors.

### When to Use It
- Deciding whether to enter a niche
- Evaluating competitive positioning
- Pricing strategy decisions

### The 5 Forces

#### Force 1: Competitive Rivalry
Questions:
- How many direct competitors?
- Are they aggressive (price wars, constant promos)?
- Market growth rate (growing pie or fighting for share)?

**Scoring:**
- Low rivalry (1-2 competitors, growing market) = Attractive
- High rivalry (10+ competitors, saturated) = Unattractive

#### Force 2: Threat of New Entrants
Questions:
- How easy is it for someone to copy you?
- Capital requirements low? (digital = usually yes)
- Strong brands dominating entry?

**Scoring:**
- High barrier (specialized expertise, brand moat) = Attractive
- Low barrier (anyone can start tomorrow) = Unattractive

#### Force 3: Supplier Power
Questions:
- Dependent on specific tools/platforms? (e.g., only on Facebook)
- Can suppliers raise prices easily?
- Switching costs high?

**Scoring:**
- Low power (many alternatives) = Attractive
- High power (locked into one platform) = Unattractive

**Solopreneur Context:** Suppliers = platforms, tools, payment processors

#### Force 4: Buyer Power
Questions:
- Do buyers have many alternatives?
- Price-sensitive market?
- Switching costs for buyers low?

**Scoring:**
- Low power (few alternatives, high loyalty) = Attractive
- High power (commoditized, price-driven) = Unattractive

#### Force 5: Threat of Substitutes
Questions:
- Can buyers solve problem differently? (free content vs paid course)
- Better value alternatives emerging?

**Scoring:**
- Low threat (unique solution) = Attractive
- High threat (many substitutes) = Unattractive

### Five Forces Assessment Template

```markdown
## Five Forces: [Your Niche]

| Force | Strength (1-5) | Evidence | Impact |
|-------|---------------|----------|--------|
| Rivalry | [3] | [10+ competitors, price wars] | [Medium threat] |
| New Entrants | [4] | [Low startup cost, easy to copy] | [High threat] |
| Supplier Power | [2] | [Multiple platform options] | [Low threat] |
| Buyer Power | [3] | [Many alternatives, price-sensitive] | [Medium threat] |
| Substitutes | [3] | [Free YouTube tutorials exist] | [Medium threat] |

**Overall Attractiveness:** [Score: 15/25 = Moderate]

**Decision:** [Enter with differentiation strategy / Avoid / Enter low-cost]
```

## 3. TAM/SAM/SOM Sizing

### What It Is
Estimate market size at 3 levels: Total, Serviceable, Obtainable.

### When to Use It
- Validating if niche is big enough
- Setting realistic revenue goals
- Pitching to partners/investors

### The 3 Levels

#### TAM (Total Addressable Market)
"How big is the entire market if everyone who could buy, did buy?"

**Formula:**
```
TAM = Total potential customers × Average price point
```

**Example (Online Business Courses Vietnam):**
```
TAM = 10M internet users interested in business × 500K avg = 5,000 billion VND
```

#### SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)
"How big is the segment I can realistically target?"

**Formula:**
```
SAM = TAM × % you can actually serve
```

**Example:**
```
SAM = 5,000B × 10% (Vietnamese speakers, 25-40 age) = 500 billion VND
```

#### SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
"How much can I realistically capture in Year 1-3?"

**Formula:**
```
SOM = SAM × % market share achievable
```

**Example:**
```
SOM Year 1 = 500B × 0.01% = 50 million VND
SOM Year 3 = 500B × 0.1% = 500 million VND
```

### TAM/SAM/SOM Template

```markdown
## Market Sizing: [Your Niche]

### TAM
- Total potential customers: [10M people]
- Average price point: [500K]
- TAM = [5,000 billion VND]

### SAM (Filters Applied)
- Geographic: [Vietnam only] → [100%]
- Demographic: [Age 25-40] → [40%]
- Psychographic: [Early adopters] → [25%]
- SAM = [TAM × 40% × 25%] = [500 billion VND]

### SOM (Reality Check)
- Year 1 Target: [100 customers × 500K] = [50M VND]
- Market share: [50M / 500B] = [0.01%]
- Feasibility: [Realistic / Aggressive / Conservative]

**Validation:**
- If SOM Year 1 < 100M VND → Niche may be too small
- If SOM requires >1% market share → Overly optimistic
```

## 4. Trend Analysis (Google Trends VN)

### What It Is
Identify rising, falling, or stable search interest over time.

### When to Use It
- Validating demand for keyword/topic
- Timing product launches
- Avoiding dying trends

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Identify Core Keywords
```
Examples:
- "học kinh doanh online"
- "khóa học marketing"
- "passive income"
```

#### Step 2: Google Trends Setup
- Go to trends.google.com
- Set region: Vietnam
- Set timeframe: Past 12 months (for recent trends) or 5 years (for long-term)
- Compare 3-5 related keywords

#### Step 3: Interpret Patterns
- **Rising:** Interest score increasing (50 → 80) = Good
- **Stable:** Flat line (60-70 consistently) = Evergreen
- **Falling:** Decreasing (80 → 40) = Dying trend
- **Seasonal:** Spikes at certain times = Plan launches accordingly

#### Step 4: Related Queries Analysis
Check "Related queries" section:
- **Rising queries:** New opportunities
- **Top queries:** What people actually search

### Trend Analysis Template

```markdown
## Trend Analysis: [Your Niche]

### Primary Keyword: [học marketing online]
- Trend direction: [Rising +30% past 12 months]
- Seasonality: [Peaks in Jan, June (new year, summer)]
- Current interest score: [75/100]

### Comparison Keywords:
| Keyword | Trend | Interest Score | Notes |
|---------|-------|----------------|-------|
| [học marketing online] | [↑] | [75] | [Primary target] |
| [digital marketing] | [→] | [60] | [Stable demand] |
| [marketing automation] | [↑↑] | [45] | [Emerging] |

### Related Rising Queries:
1. [AI marketing] — +250%
2. [TikTok marketing] — +180%
3. [content marketing 2025] — +120%

**Decision:** [Enter now / Wait 3 months / Avoid]
```

## 5. Demand Validation Checklist

### What It Is
10-point checklist to confirm real demand exists before building.

### When to Use It
- Before creating any paid product
- When pivoting to new niche
- Quarterly demand re-validation

### The Checklist

```markdown
## Demand Validation: [Your Product Idea]

- [ ] **Search Volume Check**
  - Google Keyword Planner: [1K+ monthly searches]
  - Google Trends: [Rising or stable trend]

- [ ] **Social Media Signals**
  - Facebook groups: [5+ active groups, 10K+ members each]
  - Engagement rate: [High activity, daily posts]

- [ ] **Existing Products**
  - Competitors selling: [Yes, 5+ competitors]
  - Price range: [300K - 1.5M VND]
  - Reviews: [Positive demand signals]

- [ ] **Problem Severity**
  - People actively complaining: [Found in forums, groups]
  - Pain point urgency: [High / Medium / Low]

- [ ] **Willingness to Pay**
  - Existing paid solutions: [Yes, people are buying]
  - Testimonials mentioning ROI: [Found evidence]

- [ ] **Content Consumption**
  - YouTube videos on topic: [100K+ views on top videos]
  - Blog articles: [High engagement, shares]

- [ ] **Question Volume**
  - Quora/Reddit questions: [Active threads]
  - Facebook group questions: [Weekly questions]

- [ ] **Event Interest**
  - Webinars/workshops: [Filled events on this topic]
  - Tickets selling: [People paying to learn]

- [ ] **Influencer Activity**
  - Influencers teaching this: [3+ active creators]
  - Audience size: [10K+ followers each]

- [ ] **Tool/Platform Adoption**
  - Related tools popular: [High adoption rates]
  - Platform integrations: [Demand for solutions]

**Score:** [8/10 = Strong Demand]
**Decision:** [Proceed / More Research Needed / Pivot]
```

## 6. Market Timing Assessment

### What It Is
Determine if now is the right time to enter market.

### Timing Signals

#### Too Early
- No existing competitors (risk: no proven demand)
- Target audience doesn't understand problem yet
- Infrastructure not ready (payment, delivery)

#### Just Right
- 3-10 competitors (validation without saturation)
- Growing search trends
- Early adopters actively buying

#### Too Late
- 50+ competitors
- Falling search trends
- Commoditized (price wars)

### Timing Template

```markdown
## Timing Assessment: [Your Niche]

### Market Maturity Stage: [Early Growth / Growth / Maturity / Decline]

**Evidence:**
- Competitor count: [8 active competitors]
- Search trend: [+40% past year]
- Price stability: [Stable, no race to bottom]
- Innovation: [Still room for differentiation]

**Window of Opportunity:**
- Opens: [Now — trend accelerating]
- Closes: [Est. 18-24 months before saturation]
- Urgency: [High / Medium / Low]

**Decision:** [Enter now / Wait 6 months / Missed window]
```

## 7. Blue Ocean vs Red Ocean

### What It Is
Assess if you're entering contested (red) or uncontested (blue) market space.

### Identification Framework

#### Red Ocean Indicators
- Intense competition on price
- Differentiation is minor (everyone offers same thing)
- Customers choose based on lowest price
- Profit margins thin

#### Blue Ocean Indicators
- Little to no direct competition
- Unique value proposition
- Customers willing to pay premium
- High profit margins possible

### Strategy Selection

**If Red Ocean:**
- Compete on niche specialization
- Build strong personal brand
- Deliver exceptional service
- Create community/network effects

**If Blue Ocean:**
- Move fast before copycats
- Educate market on new solution
- Price for value, not comparison
- Build barriers to entry (expertise, network)

### Blue/Red Ocean Template

```markdown
## Ocean Assessment: [Your Niche]

### Current State: [Red Ocean / Blue Ocean / Purple (hybrid)]

**Red Ocean Elements:**
- [Crowded competitor landscape]
- [Price-sensitive buyers]

**Blue Ocean Elements:**
- [Unique positioning: X for Y audience]
- [No direct competitor doing exactly this]

**Strategy:**
- [Carve blue ocean within red ocean via micro-niche]
- [Example: Not "marketing course" but "TikTok marketing for coffee shops in HCMC"]
```

## Quick Decision Framework

### Use This Workflow:

```
1. PESTEL (30 min) → Identify macro opportunities/threats
2. Five Forces (20 min) → Assess competitive intensity
3. TAM/SAM/SOM (15 min) → Validate market is big enough
4. Trend Analysis (20 min) → Confirm demand is rising
5. Demand Checklist (30 min) → Verify real buying signals
6. Timing + Ocean (15 min) → Decide entry strategy

Total: 2.5 hours to make informed market entry decision
```

**Output:** Go / No-Go decision with confidence level (High / Medium / Low)
